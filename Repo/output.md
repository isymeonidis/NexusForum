# Strategic Priority Clusters — Extracted Summary

## Priority Cluster 1: Sovereign Trusted Infrastructure

### Brief description
This cluster establishes the sovereign trust foundation for the European cloud–edge–IoT continuum. It combines trusted hardware, post-quantum cryptography, lightweight and physical-layer security, confidential computing, and resilient infrastructure recovery mechanisms. The objective is to ensure that all higher-layer federation and AI-driven defence capabilities are rooted in verifiable hardware integrity, crypto-agility, and autonomous survivability.

### Transition table

#### Phase 1
1. **R&I maturity:** Applied R&D and fragmented pilots across secure elements, PQC migration, lightweight cryptography, and resilient island-mode survivability.
2. **Policy, regulator and standards:** Early enforcement of NIS2, CRA, and AI Act creates demand for certified trust anchors and crypto resilience, while EU-specific certification schemes are still emerging.
3. **Operations deployment and adoption:** Limited to pilots, research testbeds, and selected critical infrastructure demonstrators.
4. **Market uptake and Strategic Impact:** Strong dependency on non-EU hardware and TEE vendors remains dominant.

#### Phase 2
1. **R&I maturity:** Standardised integration and industrial validation of secure hardware, PQC, TEEs, and autonomous resilience mechanisms.
2. **Policy, regulator and standards:** EUCC High certification, formal PQC migration standards, and ratified trust-hardware standards become active.
3. **Operations deployment and adoption:** Critical infrastructure deployments across energy, telecom, and industrial IoT sectors.
4. **Market uptake and Strategic Impact:** Emerging EU ecosystem with measurable sovereign industrial presence.

#### Phase 3
1. **R&I maturity:** Ubiquitous secure-by-design infrastructure and frontier innovation in sovereign silicon and confidential processing.
2. **Policy, regulator and standards:** EU becomes standards-setting leader in trusted infrastructure and cryptographic resilience.
3. **Operations deployment and adoption:** Default deployment baseline across all regulated sectors.
4. **Market uptake and Strategic Impact:** Strategic autonomy and leadership in trusted infrastructure.

## Priority Cluster 2: Cognitive Security and Autonomous Cyber Defence

### Brief description
Artificial intelligence is both the most powerful defence instrument and a major new attack surface. This cluster addresses AI as a cyber defence capability, AI as an adversary amplifier, and AI as a protected target. It covers LLMSecOps, proactive APT management, adversarial robustness, and trustworthy autonomous security agents.

### Transition table

#### Phase 1
1. **R&I maturity:** Applied R&D, siloed AI copilots, ML-assisted SOC analytics, and early adversarial robustness testing.
2. **Policy, regulator and standards:** AI Act obligations enter force, but conformity-assessment capacity for autonomous security AI remains immature.
3. **Operations deployment and adoption:** Large enterprises, national CSIRTs, and major telecom operators deploy AI copilots and analytics pipelines.
4. **Market uptake and Strategic Impact:** High dependency on US-origin SIEMs and foundation models.

#### Phase 2
1. **R&I maturity:** Federated edge agents, automated triage, LLMSecOps containment, and standardised robustness validation.
2. **Policy, regulator and standards:** Operational AI Act conformity assessment and standardised adversarial test suites.
3. **Operations deployment and adoption:** Certified EU sovereign AI agents deployed across multiple critical sectors and Member States.
4. **Market uptake and Strategic Impact:** Growing sovereign EU AI security ecosystem and reduced foreign dependency.

#### Phase 3
1. **R&I maturity:** Ubiquitous AI immune system with swarm-based autonomous defence and frontier AI security innovation.
2. **Policy, regulator and standards:** EU becomes global governance reference for trustworthy autonomous security AI.
3. **Operations deployment and adoption:** Continental-scale autonomous cyber defence becomes operational default.
4. **Market uptake and Strategic Impact:** Global leadership in trustworthy AI security.

## Priority Cluster 3: Federated Operational Sovereignty

### Brief description
This cluster creates the governance and interoperability fabric that transforms sovereign components into a sovereign European continuum. It covers Simpl middleware interoperability, runtime supply-chain verification, and pan-European NaaS federation under a “Schengen for Cloud” model.

### Transition table

#### Phase 1
1. **R&I maturity:** Fragmented piloting for Simpl MIMs, bilateral CAMARA corridors, and early supply-chain disclosure.
2. **Policy, regulator and standards:** CRA, NIS2, DORA, and Data Act obligations activate, while standards remain fragmented.
3. **Operations deployment and adoption:** Bilateral pilots, Data Space proof-of-concepts, and static SBOM disclosure.
4. **Market uptake and Strategic Impact:** Mixed profile with emerging EU ecosystem but strong hyperscaler dominance.

#### Phase 2
1. **R&I maturity:** Standardised integration, federated edge roaming, and runtime supply-chain verification.
2. **Policy, regulator and standards:** Security MIMs ratified as CEN/CENELEC standards and referenced in EU Cloud Rulebook.
3. **Operations deployment and adoption:** Cross-sector rollout, SME participation, and runtime SBOM in production.
4. **Market uptake and Strategic Impact:** Growing pan-European sovereignty and increasing non-hyperscaler share.

#### Phase 3
1. **R&I maturity:** Ubiquitous operation across interoperability, federation, and supply-chain assurance.
2. **Policy, regulator and standards:** EU federated governance becomes international model with Schengen-for-Cloud legal framework.
3. **Operations deployment and adoption:** Provider-agnostic EU trust fabric becomes continental operational default.
4. **Market uptake and Strategic Impact:** Full federated sovereign autonomy and international governance influence.
