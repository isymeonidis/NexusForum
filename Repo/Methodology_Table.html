I. Current Status
2026 baseline
SSL: High Foreign Dependency
Fragmented, academically dominated
EU R&I at TRL 3–5 for sovereign alternatives. Heavy dependency on NIST PQC process, Intel TDX/AMD SEV-SNP TEE architectures, US/Chinese foundation models for SecOps. No coordinated EU-wide applied R&I programme for sovereign secure silicon or edge-native confidential computing.
Baseline: sovereign secure silicon TRL ≤ 4; no EU-manufactured EUCC 'High' secure element in production.
Regulatory stack adopted, not yet enforced
NIS2 enforcement initiated (Oct 2024); CRA implementing technical acts pending; AI Act high-risk provisions not yet operational; EUCS non-binding; ENISA PQC guidance absent; NIS2 transposition fragmented across Member States.
Baseline: no binding EUCS; no published EU PQC migration guidance; CRA technical acts not yet in force.
No continental sovereign security infrastructure
TEE deployment limited to foreign hyperscaler implementations; PQC confined to experimental pilots; Simpl pre-launch; federated SOC at bilateral PoC stage; Island Mode not standardised; supply chain attestation manual in Open RAN ecosystems.
Baseline: 0 Common Data Spaces with Simpl security MIMs; bilateral SOC PoCs only.
Near-zero EU share in critical security components
EU domestic production of critical secure silicon near 0%; US/Asian hyperscalers dominate cloud security tooling; no EU-sovereign LLM for SecOps; SME CRA/NIS2 compliance costs unaddressed; RISC-V security products absent from procurement frameworks.
Baseline: EU secure silicon critical infrastructure share < 5%; no EU-sovereign LLM for SecOps in deployment.
Gate I → II
 R&I: Horizon Europe calls for sovereign secure silicon and lightweight PQC issued; ECCC work programmes activated.  |  Policy: CRA implementing technical acts in force; NIS2 Art. 21 national guidance published.  |  Operational: At least one national pilot per priority domain initiated under regulatory pull.  |  Market: ECCC-co-funded SME compliance support scheme operational; IPCEI-CIS initial deployments contracted.

2026–2030
Pilot Validation & Applied Maturity
SSL: High → Emerging EU
Applied R&D (P3,P6,P8,P9) · Fragmented Piloting (P1,P2,P4,P5,P7,P10)

RF fingerprinting and CSI-based authentication validated on EU-manufactured constrained hardware. ECCC-funded crypto-agility middleware research initiated per FIPS 203/204/205.
KPI: RISC-V prototypes at TRL 6–7; lightweight PQC benchmarked on ARM Cortex-M4 class hardware.
Regulatory activation creates compliance market pull
CRA hardware security implementing rules published; NIS2 Art. 21 guidance issued; ENISA PQC sector-specific migration guidelines by 2026, mapping FIPS 203/204/205 to NIS2 obligations; EU AI Act Art. 9 compliance frameworks operational; EUCS certification rollout mandating TEE workload protection; Simpl-Open launched with initial security MIM definitions; SBOM templates mandated in EU-funded deployments from 2026.
KPI: ENISA PQC guidelines published and adopted by 5+ Member States by 2026.

3–5 RISC-V secure element pilots under Horizon Europe; LLM-assisted SOC pilots in 5+ national cybersecurity centres; CAMARA API profiles in 3+ cross-border 5G corridor pilots by 2027; federated SIEMs with MITRE ATT&CK integration; Simpl security pilots in 2+ Common Data Spaces (Manufacturing, Health); Island Mode fallback tested in NIS2/DORA-compliant edge architectures.
KPI: 2+ Common Data Spaces piloting Simpl security MIMs; federated SIEMs active in 3+ Member States.
Sovereignty trajectory established; SME access initiated
SME compliance support scheme co-funded by ECCC and national agencies providing subsidised tooling and certification assistance; IPCEI-CIS initial deployments activating bilateral operator interconnection; EU secure silicon below 10% but industrial investment trajectory established; EUCC 'High' referenced in public procurement frameworks.
KPI: EU secure silicon < 10% but investment trajectory established; SME support scheme operational in 15+ Member States.

Industrial Scaling, Standardisation & Cross-Sector Deployment

ETSI TC CYBER hybrid PQC profiles published by 2031 (ML-KEM/FIPS 203; ML-DSA/FIPS 204); CEN/CENELEC JTC 13 Simpl security MIMs ratified by 2032; 3GPP SA3/ETSI TC ITS Island Mode fallback APIs standardised by 2031; Runtime SBOM mandate for critical infrastructure by 2031; classical-only cryptography deprecated for high-security EU contexts by 2033; Data Visa legal instruments piloted.

EU Chips Act fabrication producing RISC-V TEE designs in volume; EU secure silicon at 30–40% market share in critical infrastructure; sovereign European edge LLM/SLM in operational SecOps deployment; IPCEI-CIS outcomes in binding inter-operator SLA frameworks with security audit requirements; Data Visa pilots in Nordic–Baltic and BeNeLux corridors.

V. Strategic End-State Objective
2040 Vision
SSL: Full Autonomy + Export Leadership
Europe leads the global security R&I agenda
EU-originated outputs (PQC, TEE architectures, LLMSecOps, zero-energy security, Cognitive Twins) define the global research agenda. Aligned with SNS JU SRIA Phase 3 outcomes. ECCC Strategic Agenda fully operationalised; European industrial cybersecurity competitiveness self-sustaining without emergency public subsidy.
Triple accountability: SNS JU SRIA ∩ ECCC Strategic Agenda ∩ EU regulatory stack.
Complete, coherent, machine-enforceable regulatory stack
NIS2, CRA, AI Act, DORA, Data Act, Cloud Rulebook, and Schengen for Cloud operating as a coherent system with machine-readable compliance APIs. EUCS and EUCC recognised globally as reference certification frameworks. ETSI and CEN/CENELEC leading ISO/ITU-T security standardisation tracks. Compliance automated, verifiable in real time.
End-state: no compliance gap between deployed sovereign capability and legal obligation; no manual audit required.
Security-by-Design is the default operational baseline
Autonomous, self-healing, continent-wide security infrastructure requiring no manual intervention for routine threat response. Full confidential computing continuum from IoT sensor to HPC node. Zero critical operational dependencies on non-EU security infrastructure. Island Mode and graceful degradation are normative operating assumptions, not emergency exceptions.
End-state: zero critical non-EU security dependencies; Security-by-Design is default, not option.
Europe: sovereign, competitive, and globally influential
Europe is the global reference for sovereign, trustworthy, and sustainable computing security. EU-designed components dominant in European market and competitive globally. SME participation fully economically viable without discriminatory compliance costs. European security standards and AI-driven technologies exported as global reference implementations. Strategic autonomy sustained without emergency intervention.
End-state: EU cybersecurity technology export balance positive; SME compliance cost non-discriminatory across all 27 Member States.
